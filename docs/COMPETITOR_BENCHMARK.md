## Executive Summary

We researched 10+ direct Belgian competitors, analyzed local SERPs for 10 query clusters across French and Dutch, and benchmarked 12 international best-in-class websites. Here is what we found:

**The Belgian energy installer market is a sea of sameness.** Green palettes, Elementor templates, "reduce your bill" headlines, faceless companies, abandoned blogs, and zero educational content. Nobody owns the trust angle. Nobody has a named expert. Nobody teaches. Everybody sells.

**Be'energies has three structural advantages no competitor can replicate:**
1. A founder who was literally the person inspecting their bad work
2. A wide-open "pieges a eviter" content territory that no Belgian installer claims
3. Multi-service coverage (6 services) that no competitor matches with local SEO pages

**The local SEO landscape is shockingly vacant.** For EV charging, batteries, heat pumps, electrical compliance, and roof cleaning -- there are ZERO city-specific landing pages in French for any Walloon city. Only solar panels have thin competition in 3-4 cities (CB Energy, Reno.energy). This is a land grab.

## Part 4: What the Market Gets Wrong

### 1. Everyone sells. Nobody teaches.
Every competitor homepage says "reduce your bill" or "get a free quote." Not a single Belgian installer educates homeowners about what can go wrong. The "pieges a eviter" angle is completely vacant. French sites like conseils-thermiques.org and nouvelr-energie.com rank in Belgium by default because no local player produces this content.

### 2. Every company is faceless.
CB Energy has 65 employees but no named founder. Energreen claims 25,000 installations but no human is visible. Vectura's co-founder is buried in blog bylines. Green SA has zero team visibility. In an industry plagued by bad actors and scam complaints on Test-Achats, putting a real face with real credentials front and center would be a revolutionary act.

### 3. Local pages are an afterthought.
CB Energy's city pages are ~1,200-word templates with no local testimonials, no FAQ schema, no LocalBusiness markup, and no genuine localization. Belgreen's Brussels commune pages use the HQ address for all cities. Nobody invests in making local content genuinely useful.

### 4. Blogs are either dead or sparse.
CB Energy: dead since 2023. Green SA: one post every 2-3 months. Only Vectura produces current content. This means topical authority is up for grabs across the entire market.

### 5. Schema markup is primitive.
Best case: Vectura with AggregateRating + FAQPage. Nobody combines LocalBusiness + Service + FAQPage on city pages. Nobody uses HowTo for installation processes. The SERP feature opportunity is enormous.

### 6. Conversion paths are limited.
"Request a quote" form + maybe a phone number. No WhatsApp. No sticky mobile bar. No calculator. No lead magnet (except Vectura's single ebook). No progressive disclosure. The conversion UX is stuck in 2018.

## Part 5: What Feels Outdated

1. **Elementor everything.** The heavy inline CSS, JS runtime, and template-constrained layouts create sites that all look and perform the same. Moving to Next.js is not just a tech decision -- it's a competitive moat.

2. **Green = eco.** Teal, lime green, leaf green, forest green. Every single Belgian competitor uses green as a primary color. The international leaders (1Komma5, Svea Solar, Forbel) prove you can break this pattern.

3. **Stock photography of panels on roofs.** Every competitor uses the same angled shot of solar panels on a clean suburban roof at golden hour. This image has zero differentiation power.

4. **"Reduce your energy bill" as headline.** It's true but generic. Every competitor says it. It has become invisible.

5. **Contact form as only conversion mechanism.** The "fill out this form and we'll get back to you" pattern is low-commitment and low-urgency. No calculator, no instant estimate, no chat, no WhatsApp.

## Part 7: Where Be'energies Can Clearly Win

### Win 1: The Inspector Identity
**No competitor has this. No competitor can get this.** Benoit's RESCERT-certified inspection background is a permanent structural advantage. The site must be built around this fact, not as a footnote but as the primary positioning.

### Win 2: "Pieges a Eviter" Content Fortress
Nobody in Belgium produces this content. Build a pillar page + per-service trap content + FAQ schema. This captures search traffic that currently goes to French sites ranking in Belgium by default. Once published with strong internal linking, it becomes a trust moat that sales-focused competitors cannot replicate without faking expertise they don't have.

### Win 3: 83% of Local SEO Is Vacant
Out of 60 city x service combinations for top 10 French cities, 50 have zero competition. For EV charging, batteries, heat pumps, electrical compliance, and roof cleaning: there are literally zero city pages from any competitor anywhere in Wallonia. First mover takes it.

### Win 4: Performance as a Ranking Factor
Every competitor runs WordPress + Elementor (mobile Lighthouse likely < 60). A Next.js SSG site scoring 95+ is not just faster for users -- it's a Core Web Vitals ranking advantage that no competitor can match without rebuilding their entire stack.

### Win 5: Multi-Service Local Pages
No competitor covers solar + EV charging + batteries + heat pumps + compliance + roof cleaning with city-level pages. CB Energy comes closest with solar + heat pump city pages but misses 4 services. Being the only company with comprehensive local coverage across all 6 services creates an unassailable internal linking structure.

### Win 6: Bilingual Authority in Limburg
MD Solektra has Dutch city pages but no French. WOPA is local to Riemst but has zero city pages. Solar Engineering is bilingual but has no local pages. Be'energies can be the only company serving Riemst/Tongeren in BOTH languages with city-specific content.

### Win 7: Trust Through Transparency
Competitors hide behind "request a quote." International leaders show transparent pricing (Thermondo, MyLight150), specific metrics (Forbel's non-rounded numbers), and real outcome testimonials (GivEnergy's before/after savings). Adopting these patterns in Belgium would be genuinely differentiated.

## Part 8: Win Strategy

### Design

| Principle | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Kill the green palette** | Primary: deep charcoal or navy. Accent: warm amber/copper (electrical warmth, not eco cliche). Secondary: clean slate. Use muted sage only as a subtle eco signal, never dominant. |
| **Distinctive typography** | Headlines: Bricolage Grotesque or Cabinet Grotesk (geometric authority). Body: Satoshi or General Sans (clean but not Inter). Optional serif for long-form: Fraunces or DM Serif Display. Self-hosted WOFF2, subset. |
| **Real photography over stock** | Prioritize: Benoit portrait, completed installations (the Riemst 32-panel project), close-up wiring/inverter shots, team at work. Use SVG icons for benefit lists (Thermondo pattern). |
| **Alternating section rhythm** | Dark charcoal sections alternating with white. Not a single background color for the whole page. Creates visual rhythm and breaks scrolling monotony (1Komma5 pattern). |
| **Rounded, warm UI** | Border-radius 12-16px on cards. Avoid sharp corporate edges. The site should feel expert but approachable (Wallbox pattern). |

### UX

| Principle | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Founder-first hierarchy** | Homepage hero includes Benoit's face + "Ancien inspecteur certifie RESCERT." Not a generic value prop. The face IS the value prop. |
| **Progressive CTAs** | Primary: "Diagnostic energetique gratuit" (with mini-form or calculator). Secondary: phone number always visible in header. Tertiary: WhatsApp floating on mobile. Lead magnet: "Guide des pieges a eviter" PDF. |
| **Sticky mobile conversion bar** | Fixed bottom bar on mobile: phone icon + WhatsApp icon + "Devis gratuit" button. No competitor has this. |
| **Audience awareness** | Consider nav-level segmentation: Particuliers / Professionnels. At minimum, service pages should address both where relevant. |
| **5-second clarity test** | Any visitor must understand within 5 seconds: (1) this is a Belgian energy installer, (2) the founder is a certified former inspector, (3) they can get a free diagnosis. |

### Content

| Principle | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **"Pieges" on every service page** | Each service page includes 2-3 traps specific to that service (PiegeCard components). Links to the full pillar page. This is the trust engine. |
| **FAQ depth beats competition** | 8-12 FAQ items per service page (Vectura has 10, CB Energy has 0). Includes FAQPage schema. Target featured snippets. |
| **Specific numbers, not claims** | "487 installations" not "500+." "11.36 kWc installed in Riemst" not "large installation." Non-rounded = honest (Forbel pattern). |
| **Testimonials with outcomes** | "Depuis l'installation, notre facture est passee de 180 EUR a 40 EUR/mois" -- not just "Tres bon service." GivEnergy before/after pattern. |
| **Comparison tables** | "Ce que d'autres font vs ce que Be'energies fait" on service pages and pillar page. Name the industry problem, show Be'energies' solution. Devastating when paired with inspector credibility. |
| **Blog cadence: 2 articles/month minimum** | Beat every competitor's publishing frequency. Topics: seasonal advice, regulatory changes, installation case studies, trap deep-dives, comparison guides. |

### Trust

| Principle | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Founder block everywhere** | Photo + RESCERT badge + 1-line credential. On homepage hero, every service page, every local page, contact page. If 90% of pages don't show Benoit's face, we've failed. |
| **Certification badges** | RESCERT, any manufacturer certifications, insurance proof. Displayed as a horizontal TrustBar below every hero section. |
| **Third-party validation** | Push for Google Reviews systematically. Target: 50 reviews at 4.8+ within 6 months. Display count + score prominently. |
| **"Zero subcontracting" signal** | If applicable, adopt the Thermondo/Groupe Roy pattern: "Pas de sous-traitance. C'est Benoit et son equipe qui installent." |
| **Transparent process** | 4-step visual: Diagnostic > Proposition > Installation > Suivi. Show exactly what happens. Removes anxiety. |

### SEO

| Principle | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Schema stack on every page** | Local pages: LocalBusiness + Service + FAQPage + BreadcrumbList. Service pages: Service + FAQPage + HowTo. Homepage: LocalBusiness + AggregateRating. Nobody in Belgium does this. |
| **City x Service matrix** | Phase 1: 6 services x 6 T1 cities = 36 pages (FR). Phase 2: 6 x 5 T2 = 30 more. Phase 3: 3 x 4 NL = 12. Total: ~78 local pages. |
| **Local page quality standard** | Minimum 1,500 words. Unique city intro (not template swap). 5+ FAQ items. Local realization link if available. Nearby city internal links. LocalBusiness schema with city coordinates. |
| **Internal linking architecture** | Every service page links to its city pages. Every city page links to other services in that city + nearby cities for same service. Pillar page links to all service pages. Blog articles link to relevant services + cities. |
| **Electrical compliance content ownership** | Pillar page + blog articles + city pages for "mise en conformite electrique [city]." ZERO competition. Benoit's background makes this uncontestable. |
| **hreflang from day one** | FR/NL alternate links on all bilingual pages. Even if NL launches in Phase 5, the infrastructure must be ready in Phase 1. |
