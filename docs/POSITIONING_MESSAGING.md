## 1. Core Positioning Statement

> **Be'energies est le seul installateur en Belgique fonde par un ancien inspecteur en conformite electrique. Benoit Dezso a passe des annees a constater les degats causes par des installations mal concues, mal dimensionnees, ou non conformes. Aujourd'hui, il installe ce qu'il aurait approuve.**

In English, for internal reference:

> Be'energies is the only Belgian installer founded by a former electrical compliance inspector. Benoit Dezso spent years documenting the damage caused by poorly designed, poorly sized, or non-compliant installations. Today, he installs what he would have approved.

### Why this works

Every competitor positions on either **scale** (CB Energy: 12,500 installations), **longevity** (Green SA: since 2007), or **reviews** (Vectura: 4.71/5). These are interchangeable claims. Any company can accumulate years and reviews.

Nobody can claim: "I used to be the person who caught your mistakes." This is an identity, not a metric. It cannot be copied, purchased, or templated.

### The positioning in one line (for internal alignment)

**"L'installateur qui a d'abord ete l'inspecteur."**

## 2. Brand Promise

> **Chez Be'energies, chaque installation est concue comme si elle allait etre inspectee demain. Parce que Benoit sait exactement ce que l'inspecteur va verifier.**

Translation: At Be'energies, every installation is designed as if it would be inspected tomorrow. Because Benoit knows exactly what the inspector will check.

### The three layers of the promise

| Layer | What we promise | How we deliver |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| **Technical excellence** | Your installation will be compliant, correctly dimensioned, and built to last | Former inspector designs every system. No shortcuts, no undersizing. |
| **Radical honesty** | We show you the real numbers, not inflated projections | Published tariff tables, worked financial examples, honest battery advice |
| **Ongoing protection** | Your investment is monitored and maintained | Post-installation support, compliance guidance, regulatory updates |

### What we explicitly do NOT promise

- "Zero electricity bill" -- dishonest and a scam red flag
- "Return on investment in 2 years" -- realistic is 5-7 years
- "Free installation" -- code for predatory financing
- "We're the cheapest" -- we're the most competent

## 7. Framing Benoit's Background: Authority Without Fear

### The Problem to Avoid

If we frame Benoit's inspection background as "I've seen horrible things" or "most installers are dangerous," we create:
- Anxiety that paralyzes rather than motivates
- A negative brand association (fear-based marketing)
- Legal risk (implied defamation of competitors)
- A tone that feels adversarial rather than expert

### The Reframe: From Watchdog to Guardian

**Not this:** "I've seen disasters. Be afraid of other installers."
**This:** "I know what good looks like because I've evaluated thousands of installations. I build to the standard I used to enforce."

The shift is from **critic** to **craftsman**. Benoit's inspection experience isn't about what went wrong elsewhere -- it's about what goes right at Be'energies.

### The Voice Framework

| Context | Bad framing (avoid) | Good framing (use) |
|---------|-------------------|--------------------|
| General positioning | "D'autres installateurs font du mauvais travail" | "Je connais les normes de l'interieur. Chaque installation Be'energies est concue pour les respecter." |
| Service page intro | "Attention aux mauvais installateurs" | "En tant qu'ancien inspecteur, je sais exactement ce qu'un controleur va verifier. Votre installation sera prete." |
| Piege content | "Votre installateur vous ment" | "Voici ce que j'ai appris pendant mes annees d'inspection, pour que vous puissiez poser les bonnes questions." |
| Testimonial context | "Contrairement aux autres..." | "Benoit a pris le temps de tout verifier avant de proposer quoi que ce soit." (let clients say it) |
| Battery advice | "Les vendeurs vous poussent des batteries inutiles" | "Une batterie n'est pas toujours rentable. Voici comment calculer si c'est le cas pour vous." |
| Compliance page | "Beaucoup d'installations ne sont pas conformes" | "La conformite electrique, ce n'est pas une formalite. C'est ce qui protege votre maison et valide votre assurance." |

### The Three Permitted Registers

**1. The Teacher**
Used when explaining regulatory complexity, financial calculations, technical choices.
> "Voici comment fonctionne le tarif prosumer en 2026, et ce que ca signifie concretement pour votre portefeuille."

**2. The Advisor**
Used when guiding decisions, recommending (or not recommending) products.
> "Pour votre profil de consommation, une batterie n'est pas rentable avant 2030. Voici pourquoi, et ce que je recommande a la place."

**3. The Craftsman**
Used when describing the installation process, quality standards, technical choices.
> "On utilise des micro-onduleurs SolarEdge parce qu'ils permettent un monitoring panneau par panneau. Si un panneau faiblit, on le voit immediatement."

**Never: The Accuser.** We don't name or imply bad competitors. We educate so the client can judge for themselves.

## 8. "Pieges a Eviter" as a Commercial Differentiator

### Why "Pieges" Is Not Negative Marketing

The "pieges a eviter" concept sounds like it could be fear-based. It isn't, when framed correctly. Here's the distinction:

| Fear-based (avoid) | Education-based (use) |
|--------------------|-----------------------|
| "Beware of scammers!" | "Voici les questions a poser avant de signer." |
| "Most installers are incompetent" | "Un bon installateur fait ces 5 choses. Verifiez." |
| "You'll get ripped off without us" | "Avec ces informations, vous pouvez juger vous-meme." |

The "pieges" content **empowers** the customer. It gives them a checklist, a framework for evaluation. Be'energies happens to pass every check. That's the commercial mechanism -- not fear, but informed comparison.

### The Conversion Funnel of "Pieges"

```
AWARENESS (SEO + social)
  "Les 7 pieges a eviter quand on installe des panneaux solaires"
  -> Ranks for "arnaque panneaux solaires belgique" (high intent)
  -> Shared on social as consumer protection content
  -> Positions Be'energies as the transparent expert

CONSIDERATION (pillar page + service pages)
  Visitor reads the traps. Recognizes some from their own quote experiences.
  Each trap includes "Comment Be'energies fait differemment" section.
  -> The comparison is implicit, not stated. The customer draws their own conclusion.

CONVERSION (CTA at bottom of each trap)
  "Vous avez recu un devis ? Benoit peut le verifier gratuitement."
  or: "Demandez votre diagnostic : les vrais chiffres, sans surprises."
  -> The "pieges" content creates demand for the diagnostic CTA.

RETENTION (post-installation)
  "Guide de suivi post-installation : les 5 points a verifier chaque annee"
  -> Existing clients become advocates who share the "pieges" content.
```

## 9. Messaging Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- Use first person for Benoit's voice ("J'ai constate..." not "Notre fondateur a constate...")
- Include specific numbers with decimal precision ("11.36 kWc" not "environ 11 kWc")
- Name the tariff, the GRD, the regulation -- specificity is credibility
- Let testimonials carry the competitive comparison ("Benoit a pris le temps..." implies others didn't)
- Display "Mis a jour en [month] 2026" on all regulatory content
- Write for Belgian French (vous, not tu; practical tone, not Parisian sophistication)
- Use questions as headlines in FAQ and blog ("Les panneaux solaires sont-ils encore rentables ?")
- End every content section with a bridge to the next logical action

### Don't

- Never say "contrairement a nos concurrents" or name competitors
- Never use "gratuit" for the installation itself (scam signal)
- Never promise specific savings amounts without disclaimers
- Never use exclamation marks in headlines (signals desperation)
- Never use "nous sommes les meilleurs" (let the work prove it)
- Never use stock photography of solar panels on roofs at sunset
- Never use "ecologique" or "vert" as primary messaging (it's a commodity claim)
- Never use "revolutionnaire" or "innovant" (it's what scammers say)
- Never hide behind "contactez-nous" when you could show real information first
